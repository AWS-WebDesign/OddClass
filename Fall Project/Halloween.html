Halloween

Halloween is a time of celebrating all things scary. From spooky costumes to delicious sweet treats, Halloween is a versatile holiday with space for everyone to celebrate. Below, you will find DIY costume ideas, ethical candy options, and the best trick or treating places in Tacoma. Enjoy!

Spooky Costume DIYS

  1. Soda Bottle Costume

    This costume is an easy hit! To make it, gather these simple items:

    • A single-colored bright dress or shirt/pants combo
    • A pie tin (for the hat)
    • Colored paper and glue
    • Colored fabric and scissors (or fabric pens)
    • A bottle of soda pop

    Simply decorate the dress to match the soda pop of your choosing, and use fun paper to make the ridges of the pie tin hat. Use paper to give the lid a label if you want also. This is a great costume to do with friends.



  2. Sashimi Costume

    This one is even easier than the first. Here's what you'll need:

    • An all-white outfit or casual dress
    • Cardboard
    • Dark Green fabric
    • Orange and white paint
    • A plain headband and glue
    • Green paper
    • Scissors
    Measure the fabric around your waist and sew it so you can easily take it on and off. Cut the cardboard into a rectangle and paint it like a fish with the paint. Let this dry. Cut the green paper into spikes and glue it onto the headband for the grass part. Glue the cardboard's back to the green waistbelt and wear this over your white dress.



  3. Boba Tea Costume

    For all you dormers out there, this costume may represent your favorite Uber Eats Order. Luckily, it's super simple to recreate! The components are as follows:

    • A white dress, preferrably with a flowy bottom
    • Around 20 black pom-poms
    • A paper towel roll
    • A headband
    • pink paper
    • A bubble tea drink of your choice
    • Shoes that match your skin tone/aren't noticeable
    Glue the pom-poms onto the dress. For the headband, wrap pink paper around the paper towel roll and glue or tape it on. Cut the paper towel roll sideways and glue it onto the headband. You're all set! Just put the whole outfit together and you'll have a great costume.
